# Review Observations

## Evidence Standard

This file summarizes public review evidence from Trustpilot and Zocdoc. The review_coding.csv file uses paraphrased review summaries rather than full copied review text.

These reviews should be treated as directional evidence, not statistically complete proof. Trustpilot has a very small sample size. Zocdoc has a large review count, but the visible review snippets are short and heavily positive.

## Sources Used

### Trustpilot

Trustpilot showed 5 total reviews and an average rating of 3.1.

The visible Trustpilot review mix was polarized:

* 2 positive reviews
* 3 negative reviews

Negative reviews were detailed and operationally specific. Positive reviews emphasized provider care, feeling heard, team responsiveness, and easy signup.

### Zocdoc

Zocdoc showed 1,298 reviews and an overall rating of 4.85.

The visible Zocdoc review snippets were mostly positive. Common themes included:

* Provider listens
* Patient feels heard
* Care feels personal
* Telehealth is easy to use
* Explanations reduce confusion
* Patients notice mood or anxiety improvement
* Online care is easier than expected

The visible Zocdoc sample had limited negative content, so it should not be used to claim the absence of operational issues.

## Main Positive Themes

### Provider quality

Many Zocdoc reviews and positive Trustpilot reviews describe providers as caring, attentive, informative, and collaborative.

Interpretation:
The core care experience appears strong when the patient successfully reaches the provider.

### Feeling heard

Several reviews mention feeling heard, understood, or included in the care plan.

Interpretation:
Legion's provider experience may be a major retention asset.

### Telehealth convenience

Zocdoc reviews mention that virtual care is easy to use and fits into busy schedules.

Interpretation:
The virtual care model supports activation and retention when the digital and operational flow works.

### Explanation clarity

Some reviews mention that explanations made the experience less confusing.

Interpretation:
Clear explanation is a strength in the clinical visit itself. The product flow should mirror that same clarity before booking.

## Main Negative Themes

### Billing and cancellation trust

One detailed Trustpilot review described a same-day cancellation followed by a charge and difficulty removing a card from the profile.

Interpretation:
Billing, cancellation, and card-management moments are high-trust operational areas. Confusion here can damage confidence even if care quality is strong.

### Same-day appointment cancellation

One Trustpilot review described a same-day appointment cancellation due to medication-related concerns after the patient said they had previously called to confirm there would not be an issue.

Interpretation:
Eligibility and medication-related fit should be clarified as early as possible before the patient commits time or expects care continuity.

### Provider lateness and referral follow-through

One Trustpilot review described a provider being late and a referral not being sent.

Interpretation:
The operational handoff after a visit matters. A weak post-visit handoff can turn a completed appointment into a bad experience.

### Support response issues

Negative Trustpilot reviews mention difficulty getting useful follow-up or a working contact path.

Interpretation:
Support escalation is a key trust layer, especially around billing, cancellation, and care-continuity issues.

## Link Back to Funnel Walkthrough

The review evidence supports the idea that Legion's core provider experience can be strong, but operational trust moments may create friction.

The walkthrough already found possible friction around:

* Provider match transparency
* Ability to edit prior answers
* Availability language
* Insurance and cost uncertainty
* Booking confidence before personal/payment details

The reviews add supporting evidence around:

* Billing trust
* Cancellation reliability
* Support response
* Medication-related eligibility clarity
* Care follow-through

## Strongest Combined Insight

The highest-value growth opportunity may not be simply getting more users into intake. The more precise opportunity is improving trust and clarity from intake to first visit.

Working insight:

Legion appears to deliver strong provider experiences once patients reach care, but the path from booking to care contains operational trust moments that can create friction: insurance clarity, cancellation rules, card management, provider fit, medication eligibility, and support escalation.

## Audit Implication

The audit should focus on intake-to-activation, with emphasis on trust and clarity before the first appointment.

Recommended framing:

Improve intake-to-first-visit activation by making the pre-booking experience more transparent, editable, and trust-building, especially around provider match logic, insurance/cost expectations, cancellation rules, and what happens after a slot is selected.

## Claims to Avoid

Do not say:

* Legion has a bad patient experience.
* Billing is broken.
* Provider matching is fake.
* Patients frequently get cancelled.
* Support is generally poor.
* Zocdoc proves there are no issues.

Use safer language:

* Public reviews suggest operational trust concerns in a small negative sample.
* Zocdoc reviews suggest strong provider experience after care begins.
* Trustpilot complaints are limited but detailed.
* Internal data would be needed to quantify frequency and conversion impact.

## Recommended Next Step

Move to benchmark research.

Find external benchmarks for:

* Telehealth no-show rates
* Behavioral health appointment no-show rates
* Digital intake abandonment
* First-visit conversion
* Behavioral health retention after first visit
* Patient trust and billing transparency in healthcare

The goal is to support the model assumptions and show why small improvements in activation can matter financially.
